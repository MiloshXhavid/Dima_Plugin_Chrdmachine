  <how-to-verify>
Load the plugin in your DAW (Ableton or any VST3 host) with a MIDI monitor on the plugin's output.

**Step 1 — Visual check:**
Confirm the PANIC! button appears in the gamepad section row, on the right side of the gamepad status label. It should be ~60px wide, labeled "PANIC!" with an exclamation mark.

**Step 2 — Event count:**
Open a MIDI monitor plugin or the DAW's MIDI monitor on the track receiving ChordJoystick's output. Press the PANIC! button once. Count the MIDI events:
- Expected: exactly 48 events
- Expected CCs per channel: CC64 value 0, CC120 value 0, CC123 (All Notes Off)
- Expected channels: 1 through 16 (all channels covered)
- Must NOT appear: any CC121 event

**Step 3 — Flash feedback:**
Press PANIC! and observe the button briefly flash (highlight color, ~167ms). It should return to idle color immediately after. No persistent color change should remain.

**Step 4 — Normal resume:**
After pressing PANIC!, touch a pad or move the joystick to trigger a note. Confirm MIDI notes continue to output normally (no stuck mute, no silence).

**Step 5 — Gamepad R3 (optional, if PS controller is connected):**
Press R3 (right stick click) on the gamepad. Confirm the MIDI monitor shows the same 48-event sweep, and the PANIC! button flashes in response.
