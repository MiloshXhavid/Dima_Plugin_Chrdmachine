**Joystick-steerable unified-heading starfield with per-star parallax, twinkle, respawn fade-in, shooting star, and nebula blob state — replacing snow-globe random-direction drift**

## Accomplishments
- Extended StarDot struct with four new simulation fields (baseAngle, speed, twinklePhase, fadeIn) while keeping vx/vy for backward compatibility
- JoystickPad gains warpRamp_, driftHeading_, 3-blob NebulaBlob array, and ShootingStar state in private section
- resized() now stores per-star angle variation and speed magnitude instead of baked direction vectors; nebula blobs and shooting star timer initialized
- timerCallback steering: displayCx_ deflection updates driftHeading_ at 0.004 rad/tick per unit deflection
- timerCallback starfield: unified heading + per-star ±15° spread, 3-layer parallax (1.0/0.6/0.3), edge respawn with 30-tick fade-in, twinkle phase advance
- timerCallback shooting star: countdown timer (12–30s interval), 18-tick streak, warp-suppressed spawn
- timerCallback nebula: slow per-blob drift with ±0.3-overshoot torus wrap

## Decisions Made
- baseAngle stores ±15° variation relative to driftHeading_ rather than an absolute angle — enables global heading steering without RNG reseed
- vx/vy fields kept at 0.0f (not removed) to avoid breaking aggregate initialization in other translation units
- driftHeading_ left unbounded (no fmod normalization) — correct since cos/sin tolerate unlimited accumulation
- Parallax depth stratified by r threshold (>1.4/>0.8/<0.8) matching existing foreground/background layer radii
- ShootingStar initial timer seeded at 720–1800 ticks via separate RNG so first visual is not immediate on load
- Nebula torus uses ±0.3 overshoot zone to prevent large blobs from hard-popping at edges
